Life Insurance 101

Whether you’re new to life insurance, or just a little fuzzy on the details, we’re here to get you up to speed.

What is life insurance?

Life insurance is an affordable and necessary step to part of protecting your family’s financial future in the event of your passing. Policies can last for a set amount of years (term life insurance), or last throughout your entire life (whole life insurance).

We think everyone with a family should have life insurance – just in case. And we think its time for a next generation of life insurance to exist, after all do you still take a taxi cab or use a rotary phone?

Since you’re a person and not a stat, we offer next-generation life insurance that can protect your family AND inform you on how you can live longer through epigenetics. We are working to transform the insurance industry so that the science can provide you with personalized rates.

Why do I need life insurance?

Simply put, life insurance is a necessary step when you have people who count on your income. This could be your family or, in some cases, your parents. If your parents co-signed for your student loans, they may be at risk if you pass away before you pay off the loans. Your life insurance policy can cover existing debts like student loans and credit cards, or help pay for future needs like your child’s college tuition.

Life insurance might seem unnecessary when you’re young and healthy, but this is the time you need it the most. You’re in your prime earning years. By not having life insurance, you could be leaving your family without the financial stability you’re working hard to create.

How much life insurance do I need?

Good question. First of all, affordability is important. You should only buy insurance you can afford. We believe that something is better than nothing, so buy what you can afford so that you will have coverage when you need it.

If you’re not sure where to start, we have a simple formula that’ll help you calculate how much life insurance you need.

  1. Add up your salary. Calculate the number of years you think your family will need your salary. We recommend between 5 and 10 years.
  2. Add up any additional expenses. Include student loans, credit cards and mortgage. Consider future needs like college tuition and funeral costs.
  3. Add up your responsibilities. If you’re a stay-at-home parent, include the cost of replacing your responsibilities like child care.
  4. Subtract your liquid assets. For example, any savings, existing college funds and other life insurance policies. Deduct that amount from your total.
